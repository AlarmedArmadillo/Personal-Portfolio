# Content Pillars & Current Focus

## Current Platform Focus
**Threads** — testing short-form video and posts to find what gains traction. Experimental phase: ship, observe, iterate.

## Content Pillars
1. **Sales** — frameworks, psychology, real execution (not theory)
2. **Email marketing** — tools, strategy, campaigns, what actually works
3. **AI & tools** — things that move the needle, honest takes
4. **Systems** — how to build predictable, repeatable processes
5. **Entrepreneurship journey** — the real story, including what's hard
6. **Storytelling** — struggles, wins, how-tos told through personal experience

## Content Principles
- Every post should speak to someone in the target audience (founders, creators, online store owners, digital marketers)
- Aim for depth over virality — the right people over the most people
- Tie individual posts back to the bigger narrative: building proof while teaching
- Story > tip. Show don't tell where possible.
- Test hooks ruthlessly — the idea can be great but if the hook fails, it doesn't matter

## Algorithm Triggers — Use Where They Fit
Named people, tools, and platforms act as reach multipliers on Threads. Mentioning them surfaces the post to people already following or searching for those names.

**Borrowed authority** — name dropping well-known creators or business figures adds credibility and triggers reach. Use when the name genuinely supports the point. Don't force it in just to have it.

**Tool/platform mentions** — Claude, ChatGPT, Threads, Beehiiv, Gumroad, Shopify, etc. These are live trending keywords. Use when the post is actually about or relevant to that tool.

**The principle:** every variable (name, tool, platform) should earn its place in the post. If removing it makes the post cleaner, remove it. If it adds weight or reach without feeling forced, keep it.

## Repo-to-Content Rule
When a decision or change is made in the repo that has a teachable insight behind it — suggest draft posts based on that insight. The decision is the proof. The post is the lesson.
